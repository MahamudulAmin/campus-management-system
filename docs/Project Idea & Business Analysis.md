# Project Idea & Business Analysis

## **Project Title**

Campus Management System

## **Project Idea** 

Universities have many offices and departments that provide different services to students and teachers. These include the Admission Office, Registration Office, CITS (IT Support), Financial Aid Office, Accounts Office, Student Affairs, Library Services, and others. Every day, students and faculty members need to interact with these offices for various purposes such as registration, financial aid, technical support, certificates, and other administrative services.

However, in many cases these services are not centralized. Students often have to visit different offices physically, send emails, make phone calls, or search through multiple websites and social media pages to find information. This process can be time-consuming and confusing.

The purpose of this project is to develop a Campus Management System that brings different campus services together in one platform, making it easier for students, teachers, and office staff to communicate and manage requests.

## **Problem Statement**

One of the common problems in universities is the lack of a centralized system for managing campus services. Students often do not know which office is responsible for a particular service, what documents are required, or the current status of their requests.

For example, a student may need help from the Registration Office for course registration, the Financial Aid Office for scholarship information, and CITS for technical support. To complete these tasks, the student may need to visit multiple offices or communicate through different channels. This leads to unnecessary delays and frustration.

From the university's perspective, office staff also face challenges in managing requests efficiently. Requests may be handled manually, records may be scattered, and tracking progress can become difficult.

As a result, communication gaps, delays, and inefficiencies occur in daily campus operations.

## **Proposed Solution**

To solve these problems, we propose a web-based Campus Management System that serves as a central platform for campus services.

Through this system, students and teachers will be able to access information, submit requests, track their progress, and receive updates from different university offices. Office staff will be able to manage requests digitally and maintain records in an organized manner.

Instead of using multiple platforms and manual processes, users will be able to access services from a single dashboard.

## **Business Objectives**

The main objectives of the project are:

* To provide a centralized platform for campus services.  
* To improve communication between students, teachers, and administrative offices.  
* To reduce dependency on manual paperwork and physical office visits.  
* To allow users to track the status of their requests easily.  
* To improve efficiency in handling administrative tasks.  
* To maintain organized digital records for future reference.

## **Stakeholders**

### **Students**

Students are the primary users of the system. They can submit service requests, Quick communication with offices, view notices, track request status, and receive updates from university offices.

### **Teachers**

Teachers can use the system to communicate with administrative offices, access campus services, and receive important announcements.

### **Office Staff**

Office staff are responsible for processing requests, updating statuses, and responding to users through the system.

### **University Administration**

The administration can monitor overall activities, evaluate service performance, and generate reports for decision-making purposes.

## **Expected Benefits** 

The proposed system offers several benefits:

### **For Students**

* Easier access to university services.  
* Faster communication with offices.  
* Ability to track requests online.  
* Reduced need to visit offices repeatedly.

### **For Teachers**

* Convenient access to campus services.  
* Improved communication with administration.

### **For Office Staff**

* Better organization of requests and records.  
* Reduced paperwork.  
* More efficient workflow management.

### **For the University**

* Improved operational efficiency.  
* Better service quality.  
* Increased transparency and accountability.  
* Centralized management of campus information.

## **Project Scope**

The first version of the Campus Management System will include the following modules:

### **User Management**

* User login and authentication  
* Role-based access control
* Student, Teacher, and Staff profiles

### **Office Management**

* Office directory  
* Department information  
* Department information
  
### **Notice Management**

* University announcements  
* Department notices
* Important updates and notifications

### **Complaint Management**

* Submit campus-related complaints
* Complaint status tracking

### **Administration Dashboard**

* Monitor requests and complaints
* Manage users and departments
* Generate reports and statistics


## **Conclusion**

The Campus Management System aims to simplify the way students, teachers, and university offices interact with one another. By bringing multiple campus services into a single platform, the system will reduce communication barriers, improve efficiency, and provide a better experience for all users. This project addresses a real-world problem faced by many universities and offers a practical digital solution for modern campus management system.
